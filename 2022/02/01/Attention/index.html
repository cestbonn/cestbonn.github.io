Attention is all you need

Front

The fundamental hypothesis behind NLP is the belife of a universal space called "semantic". Any natural language can be transferred into the space on a uniform scale. For the task of machine translation, the only processes we need are to encode language A to the space, then decode the semantic representations back to language B.

  • RNN However, there is no way for us the encode/decode sentences directedly as a whole. We need an efficient heuristic to achieve it one word by one word, which is the truth that we reading and writing from left to right. Therefore, RNN came up. It roughly accumulates the words relationships into the current context representation as current hidden state, which is expected to be the vectors in "semantic space", and uses it to predict the next word. RNN has the drawback of precluding parallized training in batches for its inherently sequantial nature.

  • LSTM People find that not all words are important to the context representation, so that we should not simply accumulate it. LSTM introduces cell states to control contributions of components of context representation: current word and previous context. Although LSTM was originally designed to solve the problem of information loss when decoding long sentences, it indicates that we should calculate the context representation in a selective way.

  • Attention Attention is proposed to decode the final context selectively in terms of words in the translated sentence. To do it, we need weights between the current input of decoder and all words in the translated sentence. It's natural that we can use hidden states of both encoder and decoder to represent corresponding words, and then calculat the weight by FCNets followed by a softmax. This process is actually a qury-key matching. Instead of using the hidden states as qury, key and value, we use FCNets to generate them, preventing from confusing the network. \[ k_i = W_kh_i\& v_i = W_vh_i\& q_j = W_qs_j\& \]

\[\begin{equation} \label{eq2} \begin{aligned} a &= b + c \\ &= d + e + f + g \\ &= h + i \end{aligned} \end{equation}\]

Suppose we are decoding \(j^{th}\) words. Denoting the hidden state of previous words that already predicted as \(s_{j-1}\), and that of words in the translated sentence as \(h_{i}\). We call \(s_{j-1}\) a qury, and \(h_{i}\) keys.

It is designed to aggreate words relationships, so that we need a measurement of words relationship. The concept of key arises. Indeed we can use the semantic representations to calculate the relationships by simple methods like cosine similary or FCNet. Researchers however shows extra keies are suprior than those simple measurements.
